\section{Paillier Encryption}
%% ============================================================

Paillier encryption~\cite{Paillier1999} is an additively homomorphic public-key scheme based on the
\emph{decisional composite residuosity} (DCR) assumption.

\subsection{The DCR Assumption}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=blue!4!white,colframe=blue!45!black]
  \textbf{DCR assumption.} Given $n = pq$ and a random $z \in \Z_{n^2}^*$, it is hard to

\subsection{Additive Homomorphism}

For ciphertexts $c_1 = \mathsf{Enc}(m_1)$ and $c_2 = \mathsf{Enc}(m_2)$:
\[
  c_1 \cdot c_2 \bmod n^2 \;=\; \mathsf{Enc}(m_1 + m_2 \bmod n).
\]

Scalar multiplication follows similarly: $c_1^k \bmod n^2 = \mathsf{Enc}(k \cdot m_1 \bmod n)$.

\section{Threshold Paillier}
%% ============================================================

Threshold Paillier distributes the secret key $\lambda$ among $n$ parties so that any $t$-of-$n$
subset can jointly decrypt, but no coalition of fewer than $t$ parties learns anything about the
plaintext.
